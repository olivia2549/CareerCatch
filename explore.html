Start by researching different occupations online that either: interest you, you know you will be good at, or you will learn quickly. Before you get started, it will help to take a personality quiz at 16personalities.com. Personality quizzes such as this one will help you recognize your strengths, weaknesses, interests, and ideal working environment. You may find great skills to tap into that you did not know you had before! You can also take specific career tests, which specialize in assessing your talents, personality, and hobbies to find a recommendation for you. These tests do not mean that you would not work well in different careers, but they simply suggest career paths that would suit you based on your personality. Once you start thinking about the types of jobs that will fit your personality, start searching. It may also help to talk to your friends or relatives about their careers and what they think about them. Jot down notes about websites, topics, and companies that you find interesting. If you find a specific company, notice the specific wording of their website. This will help you understand what they prioritize, and therefore the culture you can expect to find in the company's environment.

Finding a job is an important decision. In some ways, the decision is binding. So, if nothing seems immediately interesting to you during your research, do not rush through the process. If this applies to you or you simply want more information before making a decision, talk to people you know who are experienced in the field. If you do not know of any personal connections, this is your chance to meet new people. Work hard in school, as this will show your commitment and help you create a good impression on people who can provide you with great connections. Constantly build positive relationships with your teachers and other authorities. Search for people to contact online. The more you put yourself out there, the easier it becomes. Plus, your work to build connections helps you demonstrate your interest for the career, which is important to employers.


Making clear goals is the final and most important step to the research process. Knowing where you want to be in the near future and why is a great indicator of the career you will enjoy. If you are the more organized type, create a list of goals you would like to achieve in the near future. This will not only help you understand why you are choosing the job, but will also help prepare you to talk about your motivations in interviews. Talk to others about these goals so they can hold you accountable to your responsibilities and help you be successful.


Once you find a field that interests you, reach out to different companies. One way is to attend job fairs. Walking up to an employer at a job fair and making conversation or asking questions can already help set you apart. You can also keep an eye out for hiring signs and advertisements, as these jobs will often end up being around your area. When you see a store with a for hire sign, it is perfectly acceptable to walk in and politely ask one of the employees if you can have a job application. You can also ask the employee questions and strike a friendly conversation in order to get a feel for the environment and your potential future coworkers.

Many schools also have places for you to visit. A school's career center is a great place to start because there will be many adults available to guide you through the research -- and later, application -- process. There are also state career centers available to many people, which are worth checking out because they can provide you with good connections and a good reputation, and can guide you in the right direction. Essentially, the best plan is to put yourself out there to as many companies as possible. In the end, it is important to remember that although you are looking for a job, the company is also looking for you. There is no rule that you must accept every offer you receive, or apply to every company you talk to.

Resources

Websites

Websites are a great way to research information about jobs that are available. This is a screenshot of the website careeronestop. If you are looking at the website of an actual company, make sure to pay close attention to its wording so that you know what the company finds important in an employee.

Newspaper

Although society is becoming more technology-based, newspapers are still good for finding information about jobs available in your area. Finding a job from the local paper that interests you is especially helpful because you will already be familiar with the area and its culture, and you will not need to make a big move.

Ads

The media is a growing outlet of information. If you pay attention to advertisements pertaining to you, you can easily find information about the different available jobs that fit you. However, make an effort to find different perspectives about the career, as advertisements often make things seem better than they really are.

Networking

Networking has to do with finding connections. Talking to people you know who work in the field will help you reach a similar job position, as these people may help you become an employee in the same company or find you a similar company and give you a good recommendation or good advice.
